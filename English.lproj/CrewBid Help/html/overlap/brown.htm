The Color Brown

The color brown is that color that you might see inside the collar of a pilot shirt that you've worn every day of summer four-day or where you've spilled RoundUp on the lawn in front of your house. It indicates that the object is tainted, spoiled, or otherwise damaged. Brown is used in the Pay column of the Lines Table to indicate that a line has overlap, as the pay for that line will probably be less than indicated, and thus is tainted, spoiled, or otherwise damaged.
